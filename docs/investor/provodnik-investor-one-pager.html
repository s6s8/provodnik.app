Investor Brief · Research Basis March 12, 2026

PROVODNIK

A lower-take marketplace for Russia's fragmented tours and excursions market.

Provodnik is built for the part of the category incumbents do not organize well enough: custom traveler demand, budget-led group formation, and regional supply still selling through manual channels. The model combines listings with request-driven booking, structured guide offers, trust controls, and a target commission of 10% to 15%.

Thesis

Executive Thesis

Problem: travelers struggle to find the right excursion for budget, dates, format, and trust; guides rely on expensive aggregators or fragmented offline channels.

Wedge: Provodnik is not another catalog. It is a request-first marketplace where travelers can create demand, join others into groups, and receive structured offers from verified guides.

Why this can win: lower commission matters only when paired with new demand capture. Provodnik competes on liquidity for custom and budget-sensitive demand, not on listing breadth alone.

Model
Business Model
Revenue: commission on completed bookings.

Positioning: target take rate of 10% to 15% against higher documented incumbent economics.

Expansion: featured placement, professional tools, and analytics can layer on later.
Launch
Go-to-Market
Start narrow: 1 to 3 regions with year-round demand and credible guide supply.

Prioritize: city tours, nature day trips, family-friendly small-group formats.

Avoid at launch: high-risk adventures, complex expeditions, and unclear regulatory segments.
Milestone
What Investors Should Watch
Core milestone: repeatable liquidity in a narrow wedge, not broad traffic.

Key signals: approved guides, traveler requests, time to first offer, request-to-booking conversion, cancellation and dispute rate.

Defensibility: more trusted guides, reviews, and grouped demand strengthen marketplace density.
